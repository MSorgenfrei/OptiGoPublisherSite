Get Your Bonus, Not More Taxes

It's bonus season, and we make sure you keep more of it.

By Mike Sorgenfrei 4 min read

That year-end bonus hitting your bank account can feel like winning the lottery... until you see the taxes. It's like finding a delicious pizza, only to discover someone's already taken a few slices. But, just like strategically grabbing the biggest pieces, you can minimize the tax impact.

"Learning how to make the tax system work for you, is a wealth unlock" — Dr. Sarah Chapstick, Finance Professor

1. The 401(k) Shuffle: Your Secret Weapon

  • How it works: Bump up your 401(k) contributions before the bonus hits. This lowers your taxable income.
  • Why it's awesome: You're saving for retirement and reducing your current tax bill. It's a double win!
  • Pro-tip: Check your company's 401(k) contribution deadline. Most plans have cut off dates, so make sure you make this change in time.
  • Source: The IRS website provides information regarding contribution limits. For 2023, the 401(k) contribution limit was $22,500, and for 2024 it is $23,000. Catch up contributions are allowed for those over 50.

2. The HSA Hustle: Health Savings, Tax Savings

  • How it works: If you have a high-deductible health plan, contribute to an HSA. These contributions are pre-tax.
  • Why it's awesome: You're saving for future medical expenses and lowering your taxable income. Plus, if you use the money for qualified medical expenses, it comes out tax-free!
  • Pro-tip: Check the annual HSA contribution limits.
  • Source: Healthcare.gov provides information on HSA contribution limits and qualified medical expenses.

3. The Charitable Contribution Cha-Cha: Giving Back, Taxing Less

  • How it works: If you're feeling generous, consider donating to a qualified charity. Itemizing deductions allows you to deduct charitable contributions.
  • Why it's awesome: You're supporting a cause you care about and potentially reducing your tax burden.
  • Pro-tip: Keep detailed records of your donations.
  • Source: The IRS publication 526, Charitable Contributions, provides detailed information on what donations are deductible.

4. The "Timing is Everything" Tango: Deferral Decisions

  • How it works: Some companies offer bonus deferral options. This means you can delay receiving your bonus, potentially pushing the tax liability to a future year when you might be in a lower tax bracket.
  • Why it's awesome: It allows for strategic tax planning.
  • Pro-tip: Weigh the pros and cons carefully. Deferring means you won't have the money now, but you could save on taxes later.
  • Source: Consult with a financial advisor or your company's HR department for specific deferral options.

5. The "Don't Panic" Polka: Realistic Expectations

  • How it works: Remember that bonuses are often taxed at a supplemental wage rate, which can seem higher than your regular paycheck. However, this doesn't always mean you're paying more in taxes overall. It's just a withholding calculation.
  • Why it's awesome: Knowledge is power! Understanding the withholding process can reduce stress.
  • Pro-tip: When you file your taxes, everything will be reconciled. You might even get a refund!
  • Source: IRS Publication 15, (Circular E), Employer's Tax Guide.

The Bottom Line:

Don't let tax season turn your bonus into a bummer. With a little planning and some smart moves, you can keep more of your hard-earned cash. Remember, it's always a good idea to consult with a qualified tax professional or financial advisor for personalized advice. Now go forth and conquer that bonus!
