Taste Naughty, Is Healthy: St. Paddy's Edition

You get the Guiness, we got everything else.

By Mike O'Malley March 4, 2025 7 min read

St. Patrick's Day doesn't have to be all about greasy pub food and heavy stews. With a little creativity, you can enjoy festive, delicious meals that are both healthy and packed with flavor. Here are a few dishes to celebrate the holiday in style—without the post-feast regret.

1. Lemon Herb Grilled Salmon with Kale and Quinoa Salad

Celebrate the spirit of St. Patrick's with a green-packed dish that’s as vibrant as it is nutritious. This grilled salmon is topped with fresh lemon and herb seasoning, served alongside a tangy kale and quinoa salad dressed with olive oil, lemon juice, and a sprinkle of sea salt. Packed with omega-3s and antioxidants, it’s the perfect way to feel good about your St. Paddy’s feast.

Ingredients

  • Salmon fillets
  • Fresh lemon, garlic, and herbs (parsley, thyme, dill)
  • Kale, quinoa, and a handful of pumpkin seeds
  • Olive oil and lemon vinaigrette

Why It’s Healthy

Rich in heart-healthy fats, protein, and fiber, this dish keeps you satisfied and energized without the heavy carbs.

2. Sweet Potato and Cabbage Hash

Skip the traditional corned beef hash and try this colorful and nutrient-dense version with sweet potatoes and cabbage. Sautéed with onions, garlic, and a dash of turmeric, the naturally sweet flavor of the sweet potatoes balances beautifully with the tangy crunch of the cabbage. Add a fried egg on top for extra protein and a fun twist.

Ingredients

  • Sweet potatoes, diced
  • Cabbage, shredded
  • Red onion, garlic, turmeric, and cumin
  • Eggs (optional)

Why It’s Healthy

Sweet potatoes are high in vitamin A and fiber, while cabbage provides a generous amount of vitamin C and antioxidants. Plus, this dish is naturally gluten-free and filling!

3. Lighter Shepherd's Pie with Cauliflower Mash

Swap out the heavy mashed potatoes for creamy cauliflower mash in this lighter version of the classic Shepherd’s Pie. Ground turkey or lentils replace the traditional lamb, making this dish high in protein but lower in fat. Topped with a layer of rich cauliflower mash, it's a perfect warm and comforting meal for St. Patrick’s Day.

Ingredients

  • Ground turkey (or lentils for a vegetarian version)
  • Carrots, peas, onions, garlic, and low-sodium broth
  • Cauliflower, butter, and a touch of milk
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Why It’s Healthy

Using cauliflower instead of potatoes drastically cuts the carbs while still giving you that comforting creamy texture. Plus, turkey or lentils provide lean protein and fiber without the richness of traditional lamb.

4. Roasted Brussel Sprouts with Lemon and Parmesan

Roasted Brussels sprouts are a crowd-pleasing, nutrient-packed side dish for St. Patrick’s Day. These tiny cabbages caramelize perfectly in the oven, and a sprinkle of lemon zest and grated Parmesan finishes them off beautifully. They’re savory, slightly tangy, and surprisingly addictive.

Ingredients

  • Brussels sprouts, halved
  • Lemon zest, garlic, olive oil, and Parmesan cheese
  • Sea salt and black pepper

Why It’s Healthy

Brussels sprouts are a powerhouse of vitamins C and K, fiber, and antioxidants. Roasting them helps to bring out their natural sweetness, making them a satisfying and healthy side to any main dish.

5. Matcha Smoothie Bowl with Avocado and Kiwi

For a sweet treat that still feels festive, try a bright green smoothie bowl. The base is a blend of matcha, avocado, and frozen bananas, creating a creamy texture and a subtle, earthy flavor. Top with kiwi, chia seeds, and a handful of granola for a satisfying, nutrient-rich breakfast or dessert.

Ingredients

  • Matcha powder
  • Avocado, frozen banana, and almond milk
  • Kiwi, chia seeds, and granola for topping

Why It’s Healthy

This bowl is packed with healthy fats, antioxidants, and fiber, making it a great way to boost your energy and nourish your body while still getting into the St. Patrick’s Day spirit.

These dishes combine the celebration of St. Patrick’s Day with a healthy twist, so you can enjoy the festivities without compromising your health goals. Whether you're looking for a flavorful main course or a vibrant side, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and leave everyone feeling nourished and happy. Happy St. Patrick’s Day!
